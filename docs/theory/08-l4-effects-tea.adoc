== The claim (as a target)

A handler is a *TEA handler* when it is:

* *T*otal — the handler's clauses terminate (the Totality Gate's reach
  extended to handler code);
* *E*ffect-graded — the effects it handles are tracked by the L2 grade
  accounting (effects are typed with grades, not merely listed);
* *A*ffine — the handler and the resources it captures are used at most
  once (grade algebra `Affine` or stricter).

The L4 goal is to prove *when* such a handler erases completely. Not
"effects are free"; rather:

> effects erase only when the layer discipline proves them observationally
> harmless.

Effects are not assumed free merely because they are typed (EXPLAINME).
The intended erasure condition is exactly the conjunction T ∧ E ∧ A.

== The obligation

[[ET-14]]*ET-14 (TEA erasure) — OPEN.* State and prove: for every TEA
handler `h` and every program `p` handled by `h`, the L0 lowering of
`handle p with h` is observationally equivalent to the L0 lowering of the
un-handled (pure) residue of `p` — the handler contributes no code and no
data to the image. A complete statement must fix:

1. the L4 syntax of handlers and the grading of effect operations (in terms
   of the ET-4 algebra contract);
2. the exact formal content of T, E, and A as predicates on handlers;
3. the observational equivalence used at L0 (see
   link:01-l0-runtime.adoc[01 — L0]);
4. the erasure translation itself.

None of 1–4 is fixed yet; fixing them is part of the obligation. What a
proof would have to show, minimally: an erasure-candidate handler can be
eliminated by a typed translation whose correctness is proved against the
observational equivalence of (3) — and the three letters are each load-
bearing (dropping any one of T/E/A admits a counterexample; the
counterexamples should be exhibited as part of the development).

== What must not be claimed meanwhile

* No README/paper/status file may list TEA erasure as proven, partially
  proven, or "mechanization in progress" unless a prover-checked artefact
  exists under `verification/proofs/` and is listed in PROOF-STATUS.
* Comparisons ("systemet vs the world") may say *target*: the README's
  comparison table row "Effects erase" correctly reads "TEA-purity target
  (open)".
* Downstream (anytype, AffineScript) may *implement* effect handlers, but
  may not advertise erasure guarantees on systemet's authority while ET-14
  is OPEN.
